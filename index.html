Hi, I'm Martín!

I'm a third year student at The University of Manchester in Computer Science (Human Computer Interaction), where I take courses in computer science, psychology, biology, and social sciences. Some of my academic interests include accessibility, behaviour, and design.

Technology has become indispensable in our everyday lives, so I believe it should be easy and simple to use for everyone, regardless of age, culture, ability or disability. This was one of the main reasons I chose to study HCI.

If you want to discover more about me, feel free to explore this page!

Academics

This September will mark the start of my third year at The University of Manchester, where I'm completing an integrated Master's degree in Computer Science with a special focus on Human Computer Interaction (HCI). This interdisciplinary degree allows me to develop my knowledge not only in computer science, but also psychology, biology, and social sciences.

There's no better example of this than the project I will undertake during my third year: Simple assistive software: design, development, and evaluation. I'm developing an Android app that will scan nutritional labels on food products and help people with visual disabilities and dyslexia understand the information, using simplified graphics and accessibility features like TTS. I have also done other projects for university, some of which you can see in my projects page.
