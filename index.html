Hi, I'm Martín!

I'm a third year student at The University of Manchester in Computer Science (Human Computer Interaction), where I take courses in computer science, psychology, biology, and social sciences. Some of my academic interests include accessibility, behaviour, and design.

Technology has become indispensable in our everyday lives, so I believe it should be easy and simple to use for everyone, regardless of age, culture, ability or disability. This was one of the main reasons I chose to study HCI.

If you want to discover more about me, feel free to explore this page!

Academics

This September will mark the start of my third year at The University of Manchester, where I'm completing an integrated Masters degree in Computer Science with a special focus on Human Computer Interaction (HCI). This interdisciplinary degree allows me to develop my knowledge not only in computer science, but also psychology, biology, and social sciences.

There's no better example of this than the project I will undertake during my third year: The Ethical and Societal Aspects of Neural Computing and Brain Simulation (SpiNNaker). I will explore how our future could change with the widespread adoption of low-level AI, and the ethical dilemmas and social implications it will create. I have also done other projects for university, some of which you can see in my projects page.
